# Client Intake Procedures

## When to Run Intake

- First time working with a new client
- When client says "you don't understand my brand"
- When content feels generic or off-brand
- Every 6 months to refresh

## The 14-Question Interview

Run this conversationally. Don't dump all questions at once.

### Part 1: The Business (Questions 1-4)

**Q1: What does your business do in one sentence?**
- Get the elevator pitch
- Note: What problem do they solve?

**Q2: Who is your ideal customer?**
- Demographics (age, location, profession)
- Psychographics (values, fears, desires)
- Note: Be specific, not "everyone"

**Q3: What makes you different from competitors?**
- Why choose you over alternatives?
- Note: Look for the real differentiator, not generic claims

**Q4: What's the transformation you provide?**
- Before state → After state
- Note: "We help X go from Y to Z"

### Part 2: The Voice (Questions 5-8)

**Q5: If your brand were a person, how would they talk?**
- Formal or casual?
- Serious or playful?
- Expert or friendly neighbor?

**Q6: What words do you ALWAYS use?**
- Industry terms they embrace
- Phrases that feel "them"

**Q7: What words do you NEVER use?**
- Competitor language
- Terms that feel wrong
- Clichés they hate

**Q8: Show me content you love (even from others)**
- Ask for examples
- Note: What specifically do they like about it?

### Part 3: The Goals (Questions 9-11)

**Q9: What's your #1 marketing goal right now?**
- More leads? More sales? Brand awareness?
- Note: Pick ONE primary goal

**Q10: What's worked before?**
- Past campaigns that performed
- Why did they work?

**Q11: What's failed before?**
- Campaigns that flopped
- Why did they fail?

### Part 4: The Practicals (Questions 12-14)

**Q12: What platforms do you use?**
- Instagram, Facebook, LinkedIn, TikTok, Email, etc.
- Note: Which ones matter most?

**Q13: How often do you want to post?**
- Daily? Weekly? Monthly?
- Note: What's realistic for them?

**Q14: What topics should we cover?**
- Content pillars
- What they want to be known for

## After the Interview

### 1. Generate Brand Profile

```markdown
# Brand Profile: [Company Name]

## Business
- **One-liner:** [Q1 answer]
- **Ideal customer:** [Q2 answer]
- **Differentiator:** [Q3 answer]
- **Transformation:** [Q4 - before → after]

## Voice
- **Personality:** [Q5 answer]
- **Always say:** [Q6 words]
- **Never say:** [Q7 words]
- **Inspiration:** [Q8 examples]

## Goals
- **Primary goal:** [Q9]
- **What's worked:** [Q10]
- **What's failed:** [Q11]

## Execution
- **Platforms:** [Q12]
- **Frequency:** [Q13]
- **Content pillars:** [Q14]
```

### 2. Save to company-kb

Save the brand profile to `skills/company-kb/brand-profile.md`

### 3. Confirm with Owner

Send summary to owner:
> "Here's what I learned about your brand. Does this feel right? Anything to change?"

### 4. Update SKILL.md

Update `company-kb/SKILL.md` to reference the brand profile.

---

## Using the Brand Profile

Before ANY content creation:

```
1. Read company-kb/brand-profile.md
2. Run creativity-engine with brand context
3. Check output against voice guidelines
4. Verify never-say words aren't used
```

---

## Quick Refresh (Shorter Version)

If full interview isn't needed, ask these 5:

1. Any changes to your business or audience?
2. What content has performed well lately?
3. Anything new you want to talk about?
4. Any topics to avoid right now?
5. Still happy with the current voice?
