# Ingenuity Award — 1-Minute Video Concepts

**Requirements:**
- Phone video is fine (doesn't need to be highly produced)
- Must clearly answer:
  1. What is the problem you're solving?
  2. What is your solution to this problem?

---

## Concept 1: "The Leapfrog"
**Style**: Simple graphics + Bridget to camera

**Script outline (60 sec):**
- [0-10s] "Developing countries leapfrogged landlines for mobile phones. They skipped traditional banks for mobile money."
- [10-20s] "Now there's another leap waiting to happen. Small businesses going straight from paper... to AI."
- [20-35s] "The problem: A tire shop owner in Mexico shouldn't need to buy a computer, learn Excel, and hire IT support just to benefit from AI."
- [35-50s] "VULKN puts AI agents on WhatsApp — where people already are. A shopkeeper chats their order instead of filling paper forms. An AI partner goes with them to job sites, in their pocket."
- [50-60s] "We're not digitizing old workflows. We're creating entirely new ways for humans and AI to work together — starting with the people technology has ignored."

---

## Concept 2: "Split Screen"
**Style**: Visual comparison

**Left side**: Paper chaos, manual processes, frustrated business owner
**Right side**: Same tasks, WhatsApp conversation, AI handling it seamlessly

Bridget voiceover explaining the transformation.

---

## Concept 3: "Day in the Life"
**Style**: Documentary feel (phone footage is fine)

Follow a real business owner (Cellosa?) through:
- Morning: Old way (paper, phone calls, driving around)
- Afternoon: New way (quick WhatsApp messages, AI coordination)

End with: "This is what happens when AI meets people where they are."

---

## Concept 4: "The Question"
**Style**: Bridget direct to camera, authentic

Start with the research question:
> "What happens when people who've never used computers for work suddenly get AI?"

Then show real examples and end with:
> "Nobody's studied this. We're finding out — and bringing AI to the people who need it most."

---

## Recommended: Concept 1 or 4

- **Concept 1** is punchy and visual (leapfrog metaphor is memorable)
- **Concept 4** is authentic and research-focused (fits Harvard vibe)

Could combine: Start with the research question (Concept 4), then hit the leapfrog metaphor (Concept 1).

---

## Production Notes

- Phone video is explicitly allowed — authenticity > polish
- Can record in English
- Keep it tight — 60 seconds goes fast
- Show don't tell where possible (even simple screen recordings of WhatsApp convos help)
