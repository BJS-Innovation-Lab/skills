**[0:15-0:35] — The Problem**

"Here's the paradox: AI could add $13 trillion to the global economy. But the IMF warns that developing countries don't have the infrastructure or digital skills to benefit. They're not just missing out — they're falling behind permanently.

The assumption is you need computer literacy before you can use AI. A laptop. Software training. IT support. But what if that's wrong?"

---

**[0:35-0:55] — The Solution**

"We put AI agents on WhatsApp — the app already on their phone. A tire shop owner chats his order instead of filling paper forms. No computer needed. No training. Just conversation.

We're not digitizing old workflows. We're letting people leapfrog straight from paper to AI — the same way developing countries leapfrogged landlines for mobile phones."
