Aesthetic Direction

Visual Metaphor: The Trusted Advisor

Think: A smart friend who's really good at reading people, giving you their honest take after a conversation.

Design References:

  • Calm app - Serene, trustworthy, premium
  • Headspace - Approachable but sophisticated
  • Apple Health - Clean data presentation
  • Day One (journal app) - Personal, thoughtful
  • Linear - Modern, capable, no-nonsense

Mood

  • Confident, not paranoid
  • Insightful, not invasive
  • Warm, not cold
  • Premium, not cheap
  • Calm, not alarming

Color Palette

Primary

  • Deep Navy #1A2744 - Trust, intelligence, depth
  • Warm White #FAFAF8 - Clean, open, honest
  • Slate Gray #64748B - Neutral, balanced

  • Sage Green #6B8F71 - Growth, clarity, positive
  • Warm Amber #D4A574 - Caution, attention (not alarm)
  • Soft Coral #E8B4A8 - Warmth, human connection

  • Some uncertainty â†’ Soft amber/gold
  • Low confidence â†’ Muted coral/terracotta

NO bright reds, no alarm colors, no "WARNING" vibes

Typography

Headlines

  • Clean, confident sans-serif
  • Consider: Inter, SF Pro Display, SÃ¶hne
  • Medium weight, good letter spacing

  • Highly readable

Language & Tone

Voice Characteristics

  • Intelligent - Like talking to a smart, perceptive friend
  • Measured - Never alarmist or dramatic
  • Helpful - Focused on what's useful
  • Respectful - Of both user and subject
  • Human - Warm, not robotic

Instead of... â†’ Say...

Don't Say Say Instead
"Lie detected!" "This section shows lower confidence"
"They're lying" "The response indicates some uncertainty"
"ALERT!" "Worth noting"
"Deception score" "Clarity score" or "Confidence level"
"Catch them" "Understand better"
"Suspicious" "Inconsistent with baseline"
"Truth percentage" "Confidence indicators"

Example Insights (How the app speaks)

High confidence:

"This response appears direct and consistent with their typical communication pattern."

Some uncertainty:

"There's some hesitation in this section. This could indicate uncertainty, discomfort with the topic, or simply thinking through the answer."

Low confidence:

"This response shows patterns often associated with uncertainty or incomplete information. Consider following up for clarity."

Important: Always acknowledge alternative explanations. Never say definitively "they're lying."

Use Case Messaging

For Parents

Know when to dig deeper.
Every parent knows the difference between "I'm fine" and actually fine. Clarity helps you understand what your kids might not be saying directly.

For Relationships

Communicate with confidence.
Important conversations deserve your full attention. Get insights that help you understand and connect better.

For Contractors/Business

Make informed decisions.
When someone's giving you a quote or making a promise, understand the full picture before you commit.

Privacy & Trust Messaging

This is CRITICAL for the adult market. They'll be recording sensitive conversations.

Key Assurances

  • "Your recordings never leave your device unless you export them"
  • "We don't store or analyze your conversations on our servers"
  • "Bank-level encryption for any cloud-synced data"
  • "You own your data. Delete anytime."

Consent Reminder

  • Subtle reminder before recording: "Remember to check your local laws about recording consent"
  • Link to two-party consent state info
