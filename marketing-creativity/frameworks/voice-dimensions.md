# Voice Dimensions Framework

Based on Nielsen Norman Group research, brand voice exists on four spectrums. These are **sliders, not switches** ‚Äî and they can shift by context.

## The Four Dimensions

### 1. Funny ‚Üî Serious (0-100)

**0 = Pure Comedy**
- Memes, jokes, puns
- Risk: Can undermine trust in serious moments
- Works for: Lifestyle brands, entertainment, youth-focused

**100 = Dead Serious**  
- No humor whatsoever
- Risk: Can feel cold, corporate, unapproachable
- Works for: Legal, medical, crisis communications

**Most brands live at 60-80 serious** with moments of levity.

### 2. Casual ‚Üî Formal (0-100)

**0 = Ultra Casual**
- "Hey!" / "Send it over" / Contractions everywhere
- Emojis, slang, first names
- Risk: Can seem unprofessional

**100 = Ultra Formal**
- "Dear Sir/Madam" / "Please submit your request"
- No contractions, full titles
- Risk: Can feel distant, bureaucratic

**Channel shifts:**
- Email to customers: 40 casual
- LinkedIn post: 60 formal
- Instagram story: 20 casual
- Contract: 90 formal

### 3. Irreverent ‚Üî Respectful (0-100)

**0 = Irreverent**
- Challenges norms, provocative, edgy
- May offend some people (intentionally)
- Works for: Challenger brands, disruptors

**100 = Respectful**
- Honors tradition, careful with sensitivities
- Never provocative
- Works for: Heritage brands, sensitive industries

**Warning:** Irreverence requires confidence. If you can't own it, don't try it.

### 4. Enthusiastic ‚Üî Matter-of-fact (0-100)

**0 = Maximum Enthusiasm**
- Exclamation points! Amazing! Incredible!
- High energy, excitement
- Risk: Can feel fake, exhausting

**100 = Pure Matter-of-fact**
- Just the facts. No hype.
- Calm, measured, informational
- Risk: Can feel boring, uninspiring

**Research shows:** Users respond better to enthusiasm than emotionless language ‚Äî but context matters. Error messages should be matter-of-fact. Welcome emails can be enthusiastic.

## How to Use This

### During Intake Interview

After voice discovery questions, rate where they fall:

```
Funny [====|-----] Serious (70)
Casual [===|------] Formal (60)  
Irreverent [--|-------] Respectful (85)
Enthusiastic [====|-----] Matter-of-fact (55)
```

### Per-Channel Adjustment

The base rating shifts by context:

| Channel | Funny/Serious | Casual/Formal | Enthusiasm |
|---------|---------------|---------------|------------|
| Instagram | -10 | -15 | +10 |
| LinkedIn | +10 | +20 | -5 |
| Email (promo) | same | same | +5 |
| Email (support) | +20 | +10 | -15 |
| Website (home) | same | same | same |
| Website (legal) | +30 | +40 | -30 |

### Per-Situation Adjustment

| Situation | Adjust |
|-----------|--------|
| Celebrating wins | -10 serious, +15 enthusiasm |
| Apologizing | +20 serious, +10 respectful |
| Error/problem | +15 serious, matter-of-fact |
| Welcome/onboarding | -10 formal, +10 enthusiasm |
| Asking for money | +10 formal, +5 serious |

## Examples by Dimension

### Serious vs Funny

**Serious (80):** "Our security protocols protect your data with enterprise-grade encryption."

**Balanced (50):** "Your data is locked down tight. We take security seriously (even if we don't take ourselves too seriously)."

**Funny (20):** "Hackers hate us. Seriously, we've seen their Yelp reviews. ‚≠ê 1/5 - 'Couldn't get in.'"

### Casual vs Formal

**Formal (80):** "Please submit your inquiry through our customer service portal."

**Balanced (50):** "Have a question? Drop us a message and we'll get back to you."

**Casual (20):** "Hit us up! We're always around."

### Respectful vs Irreverent

**Respectful (90):** "We're honored to serve families during difficult times."

**Balanced (50):** "Death is hard to talk about. We're here to make it easier."

**Irreverent (20):** "Everyone dies. Might as well look good doing it." (actual shroud company)

### Enthusiastic vs Matter-of-fact

**Enthusiastic (20):** "You're going to LOVE this! This is our best release yet! üéâ"

**Balanced (50):** "We're excited to share what we've been working on."

**Matter-of-fact (80):** "Version 4.2 is now available. Here's what changed."

## Common Mistakes

1. **Same voice everywhere** ‚Äî Not adjusting for channel/situation
2. **Aspirational mismatch** ‚Äî Writing how they WANT to sound, not how they DO sound
3. **Inconsistency** ‚Äî Drifting across the spectrum randomly
4. **All adjectives, no examples** ‚Äî "We're playful" means nothing without showing it
5. **Ignoring negative space** ‚Äî Not defining what they WOULDN'T say
