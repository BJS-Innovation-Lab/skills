12. Interesting Facts

  1. The Sonic Boom of the South — JSU's legendary marching band, founded in the 1940s, is one of the most famous HBCU marching bands in the country.

  2. The Prancing J-Settes — The university's dance team invented "J-Setting," a unique dance style that has become iconic in HBCU culture and beyond.

  3. Historic Tragedy — On May 14, 1970, during a campus protest, two students were killed by police gunfire (known as the Jackson State killings). This happened just 11 days after the Kent State shootings. A dormitory still bears the bullet marks from that day.

  4. Name Evolution — The school has had six different names:

    • Natchez Seminary (1877-1883)
    • Jackson College (1883-1940)
    • Mississippi Negro Training School (1940-1944)
    • Jackson College for Negro Teachers (1944-1967)
    • Jackson State College (1967-1974)
    • Jackson State University (1974-present)

  5. Research University — JSU is classified as an "R2: Doctoral University – High research activity" and is a member of the Thurgood Marshall College Fund.

  6. Deion Sanders Effect — When football legend Deion "Coach Prime" Sanders coached at JSU (2020-2022), he brought national attention to the university and HBCU athletics, dramatically increasing applications and visibility.

  7. 220-Acre Campus — The main campus has over 50 academic buildings, with Ayer Hall (built in 1903) being the oldest structure, listed on the National Register of Historic Places.

My Reflections

Jackson State University is a historically significant institution with a rich 148-year legacy of educating Black leaders in America. What struck me most during this research was the incredible impact JSU has had on professional football—producing five Pro Football Hall of Famers including the legendary Walter Payton, widely considered one of the greatest running backs in NFL history.

Beyond athletics, JSU has shaped American history in meaningful ways. The 1970 Jackson State killings remind us of the sacrifices students made during the Civil Rights era, and the campus honors that memory through the Gibbs-Green Pedestrian Walkway. Today, JSU continues its mission of "academic excellence in research and service," offering affordable education (under $10,000/year for in-state students) with a welcoming 91% acceptance rate. The university's cultural contributions, especially through the Sonic Boom of the South marching band and the dance style "J-Setting" invented by the Prancing J-Settes, have influenced American popular culture far beyond Mississippi. JSU represents both the historical struggle and the ongoing triumph of Black excellence in higher education.
